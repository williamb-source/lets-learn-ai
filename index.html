Your gentle introduction to the world of AI assistants. No technical background required. Just curiosity.

What Are AI Assistants?

AI assistants are tools that understand your questions in plain language and help you with a wide range of tasks. Think of them as knowledgeable companions, available anytime to assist with writing, research, learning, coding, and exploration.

The path begins simply: if you can have a conversation, you can use AI.

The Major Tools

Each assistant has its own character and strengths. Here are the ones worth knowing:

ChatGPT

OpenAI

The most widely-known AI assistant. Versatile and well-rounded with a vast ecosystem of plugins and integrations.

Strengths

  • General questions and conversations
  • Plugin ecosystem for specific tasks
  • Image generation with DALL-E
  • Voice conversations
Free tier Plus $20/mo

Claude

Anthropic

Known for thoughtful, nuanced responses and exceptional writing. Handles lengthy documents and complex analysis with grace.

Strengths

  • Long-form writing and editing
  • Analyzing lengthy documents
  • Coding and technical work
  • Nuanced, detailed explanations
Free tier Pro $20/mo

Gemini

Google

Google's AI assistant with deep integration into their ecosystem. Strong at working with images and connecting services.

Strengths

  • Google Workspace integration
  • Image understanding and analysis
  • Real-time information access
  • YouTube video summaries
Free tier Advanced $20/mo

Copilot

Microsoft

Microsoft's AI woven into Windows, Edge, and Office. Works seamlessly within the Microsoft ecosystem.

Strengths

  • Microsoft Office integration
  • Web browsing assistance
  • Windows system integration
  • Enterprise and business use
Free with Microsoft Pro $20/mo

Perplexity

Perplexity AI

An AI-powered search engine that provides answers with citations. Perfect when you need to verify and trace information.

Strengths

  • Research with source citations
  • Current events and news
  • Fact-checking information
  • Academic research
Free tier Pro $20/mo

First Steps

1

Speak naturally

No special commands needed. Simply express what you need in your own words.

2

Provide context

The more background you share, the more relevant the response. "Help me write an email" becomes "Help me politely decline a meeting while suggesting an alternative time."

3

Iterate and refine

The first response is a starting point. Ask for adjustments, alternatives, or deeper exploration.

4

Verify what matters

AI can err. For consequential decisions—medical, legal, financial—always verify with authoritative sources.
